Course Structure

  • 1 Weekly Lecture
  • 1 Weekly Lab

 

 

 

Course Evaluation

  • Assignments - 20%
  • Quizzes (3) - 5%
  • Labs (6) - 10%
  • Tests (2) - 35%
  • Final Exam - 30%

 

Promotion Policy

To obtain a credit in this subject you must achieve the following:

  • 50% on the final exam
  • 50% weighted average on tests and overall course
  • 50% on each assignment

Lectures

  • be on time for class
  • respect other students
  • Lectures will explain and demonstrate the skills and concepts you need to complete a Lab assignment.
  • Formal attendance may be kept. Frequent absence will be noted. You are responsible on all tests and labs for any material covered or assigned in class. People who do not come to class generally have significant problems completing their labs and traditionally do not perform well on their tests.
  • Classes will start on time and late arrivals must not disturb the class. Enter quietly and take a seat. Do not disturb the class by asking questions about what took place before your arrival.
  • If you missed a class, please ask a colleague, not the instructor, about what was covered. An email to an instructor about what was covered in class will get a response along the lines of "that was covered in class".
  • After the lecture, you are expected to work on the lab in preparation for the Interactive Lecture (Lab) Period.

Term Tests and Quizzes

There will 2 term tests during the semester worth 35% of the total mark. For test dates please click on the test buttons in above weekly schedulei.

If you do not write a term test, you will be assigned a mark of zero. The only acceptable reason for missing a term test is a medical reason, verified by a note from your doctor.

Missing one term test for an acceptable reason will result in the following:

  • Missing term test 1 for a valid reason will result in using your term test 2 mark as well as the class average (all sections) for term test 1 as an average for your term test 1 mark;
  • Missing term test 2 for a valid reason will result in using your final examination mark as well as the class average (all sections) for term test 2 will be used to determine the mark for term test 2 mark.
  • Missing both term tests for any reason will result in an "F" grade in the course.

Term Quizzes

  • There will be a minimum of 2 quizzes worth 5% during the semester. INT222 quizzes are unannounced and can be given any time during the semester.

Assignments and Labs

There will be 3 assignments and 6 labs during the semester. All assignments must be successfully completed in order to pass the course. Assignments are due on the date/time specified on the INT222 web site.

Typically, all INT222 assignments are due on a Sunday @ 23:59. For more information, see the "Assignment" buttons on the page.

Late submissions will be penalized an initial 20% plus 5% for each day thereafter to a maximum of 5 days. The following also applies to all INT222 assignments:

  • Submitting an assignment on the due date that doesn't work will result in an incomplete (INC) grade assigned (see incomplete below). You are better off to submit an assignment late than to submit an assignment that doesn't work.
  • Late submissions will be penalized an initial 20% plus 5% for each day thereafter to a maximum of 5 days at which time an incomplete (INC) grade will be assigned.
  • An incomplete assignment will receive a maximum grade of a "D" providing the assignment is submitted within two weeks from the original due date.
  • An incomplete assignment not submitted within two weeks from the original due date will automatically be assigned a grade of zero (0).
  • Incomplete assignments submitted within two weeks from the original due date will receive a maximum grade of a "D".
  • Incomplete assignments not submitted within two weeks from the original due date will receive a grade of a zero (0). Please remember that the promotion policy for this course is that you must satisfactorily complete all assignments.

Assignments should be prepared as specified in the assignment requirements.

Keep all copies of listings/files and any other documentation until the marked assignments are handed back. This will be helpful in case of a dispute about "originality".

Keep marked assignments (marking sheets) until you have received a final course grade in case of mistakes in recording marks. Check your version of marks against those that are posted.
