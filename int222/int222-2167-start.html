Course Evaluation

  • Quizzes (7) — 35%
  • Labs (6) — 30%
  • Term Test — 15%
  • Final Exam — 20%

Promotion Policy

To obtain a credit in this subject you must achieve the following:

  • Achieve an average of 50% or better for all quizzes
  • Achieve an average of 50% or better for all labs
  • Achieve a grade of 50% or better on the final exam
  • Achieve a grade of 50% or better on the overall course

Lectures

  • be on time for class
  • respect other students
  • No labtop or tablet is allowed in the lecture unless they are used to take notes or take course related activities. Students are required to take notes using paper notbooks or labtops.
  • Lectures will explain and demonstrate the skills and concepts you need to complete a Lab assignment.
  • Formal attendance may be kept. Frequent absence will be noted. You are responsible on all tests and labs for any material covered or assigned in class. People who do not come to class generally have significant problems completing their labs and traditionally do not perform well on their tests.
  • Classes will start on time and late arrivals must not disturb the class. Enter quietly and take a seat. Do not disturb the class by asking questions about what took place before your arrival.
  • If you missed a class, please ask a colleague, not the instructor, about what was covered. An email to an instructor about what was covered in class will get a response along the lines of "that was covered in class".
  • After the lecture, you are expected to work on the lab in preparation for the Interactive Lecture (Lab) Period.

Term Test and Quizzes

There will 1 term test during week of of the semester worth 15% of the total mark. For test date please click on the test buttons in above weekly schedule.

  • If you do not write a term test, you will be assigned a mark of zero. The only acceptable reason for missing a term test is a medical reason, verified by a note from your doctor.
  • Missing the term test for an acceptable reason will result in a grade calculated using your final examination mark as well as the class average for the term test. Make up tests will not be given.

Term Quizzes

  • There will be 7 quizzes, worth 5% for each during the semester. Please refer to the weekly schedule for quiz dates.

Assignments and Labs

There will be a total of 6 Labs worth 5% each during the semester, but not assignment.

  • Typically, all INT222 Labs are released before the date when you have lab sessions and are due at end of the date when you have lecture sessions. For more information, see the individual Lab specifications.
  • All INT222 labs are subject to change after released. It's your resposibility to keep your lab assignments up to date before the lab is submitted. Usually, labs may be updated before the second lab sessions start.
  • Labs should be prepared as specified in the assignment requirements.
  • NO LATE SUBMISSIONS for labs. Late Lab submissions will not be accepted and will receive a grade of zero (0).
  • Attendance for labs will be taken each week, and absence will result in deductions for the lab grade.

Working at Home

To work at home, you may install the following software for web programming:

  • a web browser:
    • Firefox
      • Firebug add-on for Firefox (recommended)
    • Google Chrome
  • a text editor for typing your programs, such as:
    • Notepad++ text editor (Windows)
    • TextPad text editor (Windows), or ...
  • an SFTP file transfer program for uploading your programs to our ZENIT server:
    • SSH Secure Shell Client
    • WinSCP (Windows)
    • FileZilla (Windows/Mac/Linux)
