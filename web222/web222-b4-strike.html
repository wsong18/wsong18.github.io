Welcome to WEB222!

This is an introductory course in web programming for standard browsers. Students begin by learning JavaScript, the programming language of the Web, and the focus of this course. Then, the Document Object Model (DOM) is studied, and with it, documents that use the Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) to define their structure and content. To affect and modify the appearance and formatting of a document, students learn and apply the foundations of the Cascading Style Sheet (CSS) language. Throughout this progression of topics, JavaScript is continually used to access and modify the content and appearance of documents through the DOM interface.

This website is for the WEB222 course. The weekly lecture notes, assignments will be released from the weekly schedule below. Make sure to read the course standards/policies and course outline.

Email Policy

  • Any email communication must come from your my.seneca account, and email subjects must contain the course code and section letter, e.g. "WEB222 Section C: <subject>", in the subject line.
  • The instructors' email addresses and office info are located at the "Instructors" section above on this page.
  • Send email before a day or two if you need to see your instructor in office.

Lectures

  • Be on time for class.
  • Respect other students; do not talk when your professor is lecturing. You’ll be asked to leave if you are found distracting in class. However, you are encouraged to ask questions.
  • What is banned in lecture class? Computer/laptop usage during lecture. However, you may use computer devices to take notes. In that case, you may be asked to demonstrate notes taken after every 15 minutes or so.
  • Lectures will explain and demonstrate the skills and concepts you need to complete an assignment.
  • Frequent absence will be noted. You are responsible on all tests and assignments for any material covered or assigned in class. People who do not come to class generally have significant problems completing their labs and traditionally do not perform well on their tests.
  • Classes will start on time and late arrivals must not disturb the class. Enter quietly and take a seat. Do not disturb the class by asking questions about what took place before your arrival.
  • If you missed a class, please ask a colleague, not the instructor, about what was covered. An email to an instructor about what was covered in class will get a response along the lines of "that was covered in class".

Weekly Tests

There will be a total of 10 tests (worth 5% / ea) occurring nearly every week for the whole semester. For specific test dates please refer to the weekly schedule or my.seneca/Blackboard.

  • Tests may be given any time during the class (usually at at the beginning of the lecture) and will take approximately 20 minutes to complete. No make-up will be provided due to late arrival.
  • Since the weekly contents are built on the materials covered in previous weeks, the test syllabus is comprehensive up to the week before the test week starting from week one, unless otherwise mentioned in class by the instructor.
  • No make-up tests will be given. If you do not write a test, you will be assigned a mark of zero. The only acceptable reason for missing a test is a medical reason, verified by a note from your doctor.

Assignments & Lab classes

There will be a total of 7 Assignments during the semester.

  • Typically, all WEB222 Assignments are released on a Friday @ 23:59 and are due on Sunday @23:59 approximately 2 weeks later. For more information, see the individual Assignment specifications.
  • NO LATE SUBMISSION for all assignments. Late assignment submissions will not be accepted and will receive a grade of zero (0). Do not wait for the last moment to upload as there are processing time/queuing time/transmission time from your machine to myseneca server.
  • There should not be absolutely any email submission. Check my.seneca/Blackboard for the deadline of the assignments if in doubt.
  • Assignments should be prepared as specified in the assignment specification.

What is banned in lab class?

  • Needless internet browsing and playing games are not allowed. Making video is not allowed. However, you are allowed to take pictures of the projector screen or whiteboard.
  • No Windows environment allowed during lab class. You must use Mac or Unix based machines during the lab class.

My.Seneca/Blackboard

My.Seneca/Blackboard will be used for announcements, posting marks and etc..
