For General Class Standards please refer also to the appropriate section on my home page from the link of https://scs.senecac.on.ca/~wei.song/index.html#standards.

Course Structure

  • 1 Weekly Lecture
  • 1 Weekly Lab

 

 

 

Course Evaluation

  • 2 Tests - 35% of final mark.
  • 12 Labs - 30% of final mark.
  • Final Exam - 35% of final mark

 

Promotion Policy

To obtain a credit in this subject you must achieve the following:

  • 50% on the final exam
  • 50% weighted average on tests and exam
  • 50% on the overall course

Tests

  • Tests may be given during the lab or lecture periods.
  • If you cannot attend a test due to an emergency you must EMAIL your professor IMMEDIATELY in order to receive consideration for your absence. Leave an email message where you can be reached. You will need to provide documented evidence (e.g. Doctor's note) of the emergency. Tests missed without PRIOR notification or proper documentation will receive a grade of zero.
  • Makeup tests will not be given.

Lectures

  • be on time for class
  • respect other students
  • Lectures will explain and demonstrate the skills and concepts you need to complete a Lab assignment.
  • Formal attendance may be kept. Frequent absence will be noted. You are responsible on all tests and labs for any material covered or assigned in class. People who do not come to class generally have significant problems completing their labs and traditionally do not perform well on their tests.
  • Classes will start on time and late arrivals must not disturb the class. Enter quietly and take a seat. Do not disturb the class by asking questions about what took place before your arrival.
  • If you missed a class, please ask a colleague, not the instructor, about what was covered. An email to an instructor about what was covered in class will get a response along the lines of "that was covered in class".
  • After the lecture, you are expected to work on the lab in preparation for the Interactive Lecture (Lab) Period.

Interactive Lectures (Labs)

  • Labs are available for download from ZEUS in IFS. Its path is: Root File System/Russ/IBC233. Since labs may be updated by instructors, do not download the lab until one week before the scheduled lab date.
  • Each lab will have a component to be marked. All labs will be marked out of 2.5 marks. Some labs require more work than others. Labs must be handed in or demonstrated according to your instructor's requirements and a mark will be given.
  • Labs must be handed in or demonstrated at the beginning of the 2nd lab period after it was handed out. In other words, you have one lab period to work on the lab and it is due at the beginning of the next. If you are still unclear, ask you professor directly when the lab is due.
  • Some students may have some difficulty completing the entire lab during the allocated time, therefore, it is strongly recommended that you make a serious attempt to complete the lab beforehand. The time in the lab is to solve problems and complete (not begin) the lab tasks.
  • Many labs build on previous labs. If you skip one lab, you may have to do it anyway to complete the next lab. If you have to do it anyway, do it on time and get full marks for it.
  • You are allowed one late lab in the semester. Try not to make it the first one.
