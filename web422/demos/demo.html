Attendance

Regular attendance in classes is important. It provides opportunities for seeing techniques and program code developed and for asking questions which would otherwise be missed. In addition, lecture-specific material may be included on tests, quizzes, or assignments. If any class is missed, it is your (the student's) responsibility to acquire the notes from a fellow classmate. Failing to attend a class which involves an evaluation of some kind (i.e. quiz, test, etc.) will result in a mark of 0 for that evaluation unless a legitimate reason (which must include proper validation) is provided. If a valid reason is given, the missed evaluation will be given the averaged score of other subsequent evaluation(s).

Communications

I prefer that students use e-mail to ask questions when they cannot get in touch with me personally. I check my e-mail daily and will do my best to respond to your query within 24 hours.

 

Classroom Behaviour

To ensure good learning environment please:

  • be on time for class
  • respect other students
  • turn your cell phone off during class
  • if you arrive late, or if you need to leave early, please do so quietly.
  • do feel free to ask questions relating to material covered
  • do actively involve yourself in interactive sessions in class
  • do not have private conversations with other students or engage in any behaviour that disrupts other students during class time


Academic Honesty

It is expected that everyone proceeds in an honest manner with regard to all aspects of academics at Seneca. Students are to submit their work with integrity. The value of your own work is held very highly at Seneca.

  • When submitting assignments, students must submit a signed statement that the work being submitted is their own. See http://cs.senecac.on.ca/bullets/cheating.html.
  • Cheating or plagiarism is an extremely serious academic offence and carries penalties varying from failure in an assignment to expulsion from the college.
  • Students who collude in cheating or plagiarism will also be guilty of academic dishonesty. If one student copies from another, both will be charged.
  • Helping each other is one of the best ways to learn. However, you have to avoid copying from each other in this situation. Talk all you want. Don't write anything down.
